06 — Content Filter

Every piece of content must pass all four criteria. If not — don't create it.

1

Decision

Does this help the reader make a decision? Content exists to reduce uncertainty. If it doesn't enable a choice — it's noise.

2

Structure

Does this show how something is built or organized? Structure signals competence. Inspiration without architecture is decoration.

3

Insight

Does this reveal something non-obvious? Generic information is available everywhere. Insight is earned through operational experience.

4

Optimized

Is this built for its channel and purpose? A proposal is not social content. An Instagram post is not a case study. Format follows function.

This is not a content brand. This is a structured, high-trust business. Instagram is a positioning tool, not a volume channel. Every post is evidence of operational thinking — not aesthetic output.

07 — Non-Negotiables

The rules that protect the brand and the client.

Do not build visibility without backend readiness. The brand must be able to deliver on what it signals.

Do not work with unvalidated suppliers. Every vendor in the network has a documented performance record.

Do not design from scratch every time. If it's repeatable, it becomes a system. Document after twice. Systematize after three.

Do not say yes to misaligned clients. The client profile is specific. A mismatch creates friction that the brand is designed to eliminate.

Every client interaction must build trust, reduce uncertainty, and reinforce expertise. No exceptions, no casual exceptions.

If it doesn't improve structure, positioning, or revenue — it is not a priority. Protect the calendar accordingly.

Final principle
Everything you build must reduce friction, increase clarity, and reinforce control. When executed consistently, the brand feels clear, elevated, reliable, and different. And our objective is conversion — always.
